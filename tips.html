How to Water Young Trees
Deep, infrequent watering builds strong root systems. Follow this schedule:

Pruning
Pruning for Shape & Health
Prune during the dry season when trees are least stressed. Key rules:
  • Remove dead, diseased or crossing branches first
  • Never remove more than 25% of canopy at once
  • Cut at 45¬∞ just above an outward-facing bud
  • Seal cuts over 3cm diameter with pruning sealant
  • Remove all root suckers below the graft union
  • Clean tools with methylated spirit between trees

Mulch ‚Äî Your Secret Weapon
A proper mulch ring is worth more than any fertiliser for young trees.
  • Apply 5‚Äì8cm deep around the tree
  • Keep mulch 15cm clear of the trunk ‚Äî prevents rot
  • Spread to the drip line (edge of canopy)
  • Use dry grass, wood chips or coffee husks
  • Replenish every 3‚Äì4 months as it decomposes

Fertilising
Feeding Your Trees Right
Apply fertiliser at the start of each rainy season, never in dry soil.
  • DAP ‚Äî at planting and year 1 establishment
  • NPK 17:17:17 ‚Äî general annual feeding
  • CAN (Calcium Ammonium Nitrate) ‚Äî top-dressing in rains
  • Foliar spray (Boron, Zinc, Manganese) ‚Äî every 6 weeks
  • Broadcast around the drip line, not at the trunk
  • Water immediately after any fertiliser application

Pest Control
Common Pests & How to Beat Them
Prevention beats cure. Monitor weekly in the first two years.
  • Aphids: Spray with soapy water or neem oil solution
  • Scale insects: Methylated spirit on a cloth, then neem spray
  • Termites: Drench soil around base with Dursban solution
  • Caterpillars: Hand-pick or use Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt)
  • Fungal leaf spots: Reduce overhead watering, apply copper fungicide
