üåø Tree Care

For the first 8-12 weeks, water every 2-3 days during dry weather. The goal is to keep the root zone moist but not waterlogged. After 3 months, most trees are sufficiently established to tolerate short dry spells, though fruit trees benefit from continued irrigation during dry seasons to maintain fruit development.

Yellow leaves have several possible causes: overwatering (most common), underwatering, nitrogen deficiency, iron chlorosis (in alkaline soils), or pest damage. The pattern of yellowing can help identify the cause ‚Äî yellowing between the veins suggests nutrient deficiency, while overall pale yellowing often indicates waterlogging. Call or WhatsApp us with a photo and we'll diagnose it for you.

For most fruit trees, apply a balanced fertiliser (such as NPK 17:17:17) twice per year at the start of each rainy season. For mature, fruiting trees, switch to a potassium-rich fertiliser (such as NPK 6:12:24) to encourage fruit set and quality. Always apply fertiliser to moist soil and water in well ‚Äî never apply to dry ground, which can burn roots.
